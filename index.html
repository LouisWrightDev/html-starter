The trio of puffins – by Steve Cole

Colourful and charismatic, could the plucky puffins be speaking to us?

The decline in puffin numbers is alerting us to several imbalances nature. The small sand eels they eat are reducing in number and shifting range due to warming oceans and over-fishing. And the recent explosion of bird flu has hit seabirds like the puffin hard, and combined with plastic pollution and oil spills they’ve had a hard time. Their history tells us something else. As well as being terrifically fun to watch, puffins are apparently also tasty, and easy to catch, but many populations recovered from hunting in centuries past, once they were protected. That’s the puffins showing us that if we bother to listen to the alarms, we can find solutions.
